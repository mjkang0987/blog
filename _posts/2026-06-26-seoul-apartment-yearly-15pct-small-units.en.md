Over the past year, Seoul apartment prices have climbed more than 15%. That's the gain from June 2025 to May 2026 by real estate analytics firm R114's count, and the uptrend held even as the government rolled out three rounds of tough cooling measures. With lending rules tightening, smaller units — which carry a lighter financing burden — far outpaced larger ones, and some non-Gangnam districts such as Gwangjin, Dongjak and Seongdong jumped more than 20% in a single year.

**TL;DR**

- Over the past year (June 2025–May 2026), Seoul apartment prices rose more than 15%; nationwide, prices were up about 9% (R114).
- The rally persisted despite three rounds of tough measures. Of Seoul's 25 districts, 18 surpassed their prior peaks, and the annual gain is reported to exceed that of the previous administration's tenure.
- Tighter lending rules left smaller units far ahead of larger ones. In the four Gangnam districts, the 40–60㎡ (exclusive area) bracket rose 4.0% this year, the top gain by size.

## What happened?

Seoul apartment prices rose more than 15% in a single year. According to R114, Seoul apartment prices climbed over 15% from June 2025 to May 2026, while nationwide prices rose about 9% in the same period. The government has rolled out three rounds of strong property measures, yet the upward trend — centered on Seoul and the broader capital region — has barely cooled.

The gains weren't confined to Gangnam. Some non-Gangnam districts — Gwangjin, Dongjak, Jung, Gangdong and Seongdong — saw increases of more than 20% within a year. As demand concentrated on areas near major job hubs or with transit catalysts, so-called "live-near-work" locations pushed prices higher.

## Why did smaller units rise more?

The defining feature of this rally is divergence by unit size. As lending rules tightened, smaller units — which require a lighter financing load — saw price gains that far outpaced larger ones, even within Seoul. According to one report, as of the fourth week of June the sale-price index for Seoul units of 40–60㎡ (exclusive area) was up 6.72% from the end of last year, while units above 135㎡ rose just 2.09%.

Korea Real Estate Board data shows the same pattern. This year, in the four Gangnam districts, the 40–60㎡ bracket rose 4.0%, the top gain by size. With loan limits capped, end-users flocked to mid-to-small units that carry a smaller total price tag — making the "smaller homes rise faster" phenomenon increasingly clear.

## Why didn't the curbs work?

Over the past year the government rolled out three rounds of tough measures, yet they failed to halt price gains. If anything, 18 of Seoul's 25 districts pushed back above their prior peaks, and analysts say the annual increase exceeds that of the previous administration's tenure. Seoul's average apartment price had already crossed the 1.5 billion won mark late last year.

Several factors are cited for why the curbs failed to hold prices down: lingering worries about a supply shortage, end-user demand shifting from jeonse leases to outright purchases, and expectations of rate cuts — together fostering a "buy before it's too late" mood. The tighter the lending rules, the more those with financing capacity concentrated on a single prime home, which also propped up prices.

## What comes next?

The immediate variable is the tax overhaul and supply-expansion debate flagged for next month. The question is whether fresh measures will actually shift transactions and prices, or whether the current pattern — curbs and gains moving together — will persist. On the supply side, the pace of redevelopment and new move-in volumes will matter; on the demand side, the intensity of lending rules and the direction of the base rate are seen as the key swing factors.

If you're looking to buy, keep in mind that the trend splits by size and by district. Even within Seoul, the temperature gap between small and large units, and between Gangnam and non-Gangnam, is clear — so rather than reading only the "Seoul prices rose" average, it's worth examining the individual trend for your target area and unit size.

## The bottom line

This past year boils down to two themes: a market that rises despite curbs, and a size divergence created by lending rules. Even with repeated strong measures, most of Seoul recovered its prior peaks, and within that, lighter-burden smaller units led the climb. In the short term, the strength of next month's tax and supply measures — and how the market receives them — will matter; over the medium term, supply volumes and the rate path will likely set the direction. Whether buying or selling, it's worth checking the data for "your district and your unit size" beyond the headline average.
