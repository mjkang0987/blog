Apartments in Hwaseong Dongtan-gu have risen 11.38% year-to-date as of June 22 (Korea Real Estate Board data), topping every municipality in South Korea. In the same week, Seoul jeonse (lease deposit) prices recorded their largest weekly gain since October 2013 — a span of roughly 12 years and 8 months. Starting this Friday (June 27), the public subscription for the 3rd-generation new town project in Incheon Gyeyang Block A9 opens.

## TL;DR

- Hwaseong Dongtan-gu up 11.38% YTD (June 22 basis) — first time in double digits; +1.65% for the week, still the highest nationally
- Seoul apartment sales prices +0.30% (72nd consecutive weekly gain); jeonse +0.35% (largest weekly gain since October 2013)
- Incheon Gyeyang A9 public subscription opens June 27–28 (317 units, 55㎡, KRW 460–490 million)

---

## Dongtan Tops 11% YTD, and the Semiconductor Belt Is Spreading

According to the Korea Real Estate Board's Week 4 of June (June 22 basis) data released June 25, Dongtan-gu in Hwaseong City has now accumulated an 11.38% price gain since the start of 2026 — crossing the double-digit mark for the first time since the district began being tracked separately in February.

On a weekly basis, the gains have been: +1.98% (Week 2) → +2.22% (Week 3) → +1.65% (Week 4). While the pace eased slightly, Dongtan-gu still led every city, county, and district in South Korea for the fourth straight week. In early June, an 84㎡ unit at Dongtan-yeok Lotte Castle sold for KRW 2.225 billion (approx. USD 1.6 million), setting a new area record.

The underlying driver is end-user demand tied to proximity to the "semiconductor belt" — Samsung Electronics' Hwaseong campus, SK Hynix, and the planned Yongin semiconductor cluster. The heat is spreading to adjacent Gyeonggi cities: Seongnam Jungwon-gu (+0.59%), Anyang Dongan-gu (+0.49%), Seongnam Sujeong-gu (+0.46%), Seongnam Bundang-gu (+0.42%), Suwon Yeongtong-gu (+0.41%), and Yongin Suji-gu (+0.38%) all posted notable weekly gains. Gyeonggi Province as a whole rose +0.19% for the week.

Authorities are reviewing whether to designate Dongtan as an "adjustment target zone" or "speculation-overheated zone," citing that the area already meets the quantitative thresholds for both classifications.

## Seoul: 72 Straight Weeks Up, Jeonse at a 12-Year 8-Month High

Seoul apartment sales prices rose 0.30% week-over-week in the June 22 week — extending an unbroken streak that began in early February 2025 to 72 consecutive weeks. By district, Dobong-gu (+0.46%), Seongbuk-gu (+0.41%), and Guro-gu (+0.41%) paced the gains. The 14 Gangbuk (northern) districts averaged +0.33%; the 11 Gangnam (southern) districts averaged +0.28%.

Nationally, prices were up +0.10%; the Capital Region logged +0.20%. Provincial areas remained flat at 0.00% for the fourth straight week, a continuing reminder of the widening gap between the Capital Region and the rest of the country.

Seoul jeonse prices climbed 0.35% — the sharpest single-week gain since the third week of October 2013, a 12-year, 8-month record. The cumulative YTD gain for Seoul jeonse now stands at 4.79%. Seongdong-gu and Seongbuk-gu (each +0.55%) and Guro-gu (+0.54%) were the standout performers. Incheon rose +0.04% and Gyeonggi +0.19% for the week.

## Incheon Gyeyang A9 New Town Subscription Opens This Friday

LH (Korea Land and Housing Corporation) will begin accepting applications for the Block A9 Newlywed Hope Town at the Incheon Gyeyang 3rd-generation new town site on June 27–28 (Friday–Saturday). The offering covers 317 units of 55㎡ exclusively, targeting newlywed and soon-to-be-married couples. The sales price range is approximately KRW 460–490 million per unit. Move-in is expected in February 2029, with a 3-year resale restriction.

As a public housing project, the subscription comes with access to low-interest mortgage products. The price point is competitive relative to nearby private-market units. Applications are through the MyHome Portal (myhome.go.kr) or designated application channels. Verify all eligibility requirements and exact dates in the official offering notice before applying.

## Regional Mortgage Stress DSR Phase 3 Extended to Year-End

On June 18, the Financial Services Commission (FSC) extended the exemption from Stress DSR Phase 3 for regional (non-metropolitan) mortgage loans through December 31, 2026. The move is intended to prevent further suppression of already-weak regional housing markets, given the declining share of regional mortgage originations in total new household lending.

Capital Region and regulated-area mortgages remain under full Phase 3 rules (stress rate: +1.5 percentage points, application ratio: 100%). The Lee Jae-myung government is expected to release a comprehensive property tax reform package in July, including a stricter Comprehensive Real Estate Tax for non-occupant multi-homeowners. Details will be announced at that time.

## Outlook

Dongtan's 11%-plus rise is grounded in real semiconductor-sector demand, which distinguishes it from purely speculative surges. Even so, a weekly pace near 2% is historically unusual — enough to prompt regulatory scrutiny. A zone designation, if it comes, could moderate near-term price gains while potentially pushing demand into adjacent, non-regulated towns (the "balloon effect").

The opening of 3rd-generation new town subscriptions this week adds another variable to watch. And when the July property tax reform lands, the key question will be whether multi-homeowners respond by selling (boosting supply) or by shifting to jeonse and monthly rentals (adding pressure to the tenant market) — a dynamic that will likely set the tone for the second half of 2026.
